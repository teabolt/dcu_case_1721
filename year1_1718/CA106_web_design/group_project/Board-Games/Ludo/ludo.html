Ludo

Objective of the Game

Ludo is played on a board with each corner showing a different coloured play area. The overall objective of the game is to move around the board area with all pieces until they are in the home area of the game board. The main skill in Ludo is to know when they move their piece and which piece. The overall objective of Ludo is to get all of your pieces into the home stretch.

Rules

  • Each player begins with four pieces in their starting area corresponding to their colour.
  • To move a piece onto the playing arena, you must roll a six on a standard six sided die.
  • Subsequently, each player moves their piece by the amount of squares they roll on a die.
  • If a player rolls a six, they have a choice on whether to move six spaces or to bring another one of their pieces onto the playing arena.
  • A player who rolls a six also gets another roll however, if they roll a six on three consecutive occasions, they get a foul by a forfeit of their next go.
  • A player wins after they have all four of their pieces in the home arena at the centre of the board.
  • In order to get a piece in the home arena, a player must roll the exact number needed to enter the arena, if they roll more than needed, the piece begins to move backwards by the amount of extra positions that were rolled on the die. (for example if the last square is 5 spaces away, the player must roll a 5 to get there)
  • A player has to take their own colours path to the home arena.
  • This game is designed for 2-4 players.

How to Play

  • A player must roll a six to get one of their pieces out on the playing board.
  • When a player rolls a six during the game, they have a choice either to move a piece on the board or bring another piece onto the board.
  • A player only gets one roll per turn unless they roll a six, then they get another roll.
  • A player has to get around the whole board before being able to try move into the home arena.
  • A players piece that gets overtaken by another players piece must return to the players starting area and the player must roll a six to be able to get the piece back out onto the board.

Winning at Ludo

The first person to get all four of their playing pieces to the home arena is the winner.
